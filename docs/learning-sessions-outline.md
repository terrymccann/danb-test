# DANB CDA Learning Sessions — Complete Outline

Each session is a 7-phase deep dive into a single concept (~10–15 min). Sessions are organized by domain and sub-domain, mapped to the official DANB exam outlines. The `*` marks the one session already built.

---

## GC — General Chairside (23 sessions)

### I. Evaluation (17%)

1. **Vital Signs & Baseline Measurements** — blood pressure, pulse, respiration, temperature; normal ranges; when to alert the dentist
2. **Medical History Review & Risk Assessment** — ASA classification, identifying contraindications, medication interactions, premedication protocols
3. **Dental Charting & Tooth Numbering** — Universal, Palmer, and ISO numbering systems; charting symbols; existing vs. planned treatment notation
4. **Tooth Anatomy & Morphology** — crown, root, pulp; types of teeth; surfaces (mesial, distal, buccal, lingual, occlusal); primary vs. permanent dentition
5. **Head & Neck Anatomy** — bones of the skull, TMJ, salivary glands, muscles of mastication, cranial nerves relevant to dentistry
6. **Occlusion & Malocclusion** — Angle's classification (Class I, II, III), crossbite, overbite, overjet; functional vs. centric occlusion
7. **Medical Emergencies in the Dental Office** — syncope, anaphylaxis, cardiac arrest, diabetic emergencies, seizures, aspiration; emergency kit contents; response protocols

### II. Patient Management & Administration (17%)

8. **Oral Health Education & Prevention** — brushing/flossing instruction, fluoride application, sealants, dietary counseling, tobacco cessation
9. **Legal & Regulatory Compliance** — scope of practice, informed consent, patient rights, malpractice, mandated reporting, dental practice act
10. **HIPAA & Patient Records** — protected health information, release of records, electronic records, privacy rule, security rule
11. **Patient Communication & Behavior Management** — anxious patients, pediatric behavior guidance, special needs patients, cultural sensitivity, tell-show-do

### III. Chairside Dentistry (50%)

12. **Four-Handed Dentistry & Ergonomics** — operator/assistant positioning, instrument transfer techniques, zones of activity, fulcrum placement
13. **Instrument Identification & Function** — hand instruments (explorers, excavators, condensers, carvers), rotary instruments (burs), handpieces (high-speed, low-speed, electric)
14. **Restorative Procedures** — Class I–VI cavity preparations, amalgam placement, composite placement, liner/base placement, matrix band/retainer systems
15. **Endodontic Procedures** — pulpotomy, pulpectomy, root canal therapy, files and reamers, irrigation, obturation, rubber dam for endo
16. **Periodontic Procedures** — scaling, root planing, gingivectomy, osseous surgery, periodontal dressings, probing and charting
17. **Prosthodontic Procedures** — crown and bridge, impression taking, shade selection, temporary fabrication, cementation, removable prosthetics
18. **Orthodontic Procedures** — bracket bonding, archwire placement, ligature ties, band cementation, separator placement, appliance types
19. **Oral Surgery Procedures** — forceps extractions, surgical extractions, suturing, hemostasis, post-operative instructions, biopsy assistance
20. **Pediatric Dentistry** — primary dentition procedures, stainless steel crowns, space maintainers, pulp therapy, behavior management techniques
21. **Anesthesia & Pain Management** — topical anesthetics, local anesthesia types (infiltration, block), syringe assembly, aspiration, complications (paresthesia, hematoma)
22. **Dental Dam & Moisture Control** — dam placement, clamp selection, hole punching, ligatures, cotton roll isolation, HVE and saliva ejector technique
23. **Tray Setup & Procedural Sequencing** — standard setups for common procedures, anticipating instrument needs, maintaining the chain of asepsis during procedures

### IV. Dental Materials (16%)

24. **Amalgam & Direct Restorative Materials** — composition, trituration, condensation, carving, burnishing; mercury hygiene; advantages/disadvantages
25. **Composite & Bonding Systems** — light-cured vs. self-cured, acid etching, bonding agents, layering technique, shade matching, finishing and polishing
26. **Dental Cements & Liners** — zinc phosphate, glass ionomer, resin-modified glass ionomer, zinc oxide eugenol, calcium hydroxide; mixing techniques and uses
27. **Impression Materials & Gypsum** — alginate, polyvinyl siloxane, polyether; mixing, loading trays, disinfection; dental stone, die stone, plaster of Paris
28. **Temporary Materials & Waxes** — temporary crown fabrication, temporary cement, provisional materials; utility wax, baseplate wax, sticky wax

## RHS — Radiation Health & Safety (18 sessions)

### I. Purpose and Technique (50%)

29. **Paralleling Technique** — XCP instrument placement, beam alignment, film/sensor positioning for anterior and posterior periapicals; advantages over bisecting
30. **Bisecting Angle Technique** — angle of bisection, vertical angulation rules, when to use bisecting vs. paralleling, common errors
31. **Bitewing Radiographs** — horizontal vs. vertical bitewings, sensor placement, beam alignment, detecting interproximal caries, alveolar bone levels
32. **Occlusal & Panoramic Radiography** — occlusal technique for maxillary/mandibular, panoramic machine positioning, patient preparation, common panoramic errors (ghost images, lead apron artifact)
33. **Digital Radiography** — CCD vs. CMOS sensors, PSP plates, advantages over film, image manipulation (contrast, brightness, inversion), storage and retrieval
34. **Film Processing & Darkroom Procedures** — manual processing (develop, rinse, fix, wash, dry), automatic processing, time-temperature method, darkroom errors, safe light testing
35. **Image Evaluation & Mounting** — diagnostic quality criteria, identifying errors (elongation, foreshortening, cone-cut, overlap), dot placement, mounting in labial view
36. **Anatomical Landmarks on Radiographs** — maxillary landmarks (maxillary sinus, nasal fossa, incisive foramen, zygomatic process), mandibular landmarks (mental foramen, mandibular canal, genial tubercles, mylohyoid ridge)
37. **Exposure Settings & Image Quality** — milliamperage, kilovoltage, exposure time; density vs. contrast; inverse square law; effect of each setting on the image
38. **Patient Positioning & Common Errors** — patient preparation, mid-sagittal and occlusal plane alignment, error identification and correction strategies

### II. Radiation Characteristics and Protection (25%)

39. **Radiation Physics Fundamentals** — x-ray production, properties of x-rays, types of radiation (primary, scatter, leakage), electromagnetic spectrum, photon interactions with matter
40. **Biological Effects of Radiation** — somatic vs. genetic effects, deterministic vs. stochastic effects, radiosensitivity of tissues, latent period, cumulative effects
41. **Dose Limits & ALARA Principle** — MPD for occupational and public exposure, pregnant operators, dose equivalent units (rem, sievert), ALARA strategies, dose reduction techniques
42. **Protective Equipment & Monitoring** — lead apron, thyroid collar, position indicating device (PID), operator positioning, film speed selection, dosimeter badges, quality assurance protocols

### III. Infection Prevention and Control — Radiology-Specific (25%)

43. **Barrier Techniques in Radiography** — sensor barriers, tube head covers, chair covers, work surface barriers, barriers for panoramic units
44. **Equipment Disinfection & Sensor Handling** — disinfection of non-barrier surfaces, sensor care (CCD vs. PSP), daylight loader protocol, contamination prevention workflow
45. **Radiographic Infection Control Workflow** — complete aseptic workflow from patient seating through image processing, glove changes, contamination zones, cross-contamination prevention
46. **Darkroom & Digital Sensor Asepsis** — film handling with contaminated gloves, PSP plate disinfection, scanner disinfection, maintaining digital sensor integrity during disinfection

## ICE — Infection Control (18 sessions)

### I. Prevention of Disease Transmission (20%)

47. **Chain of Infection & Modes of Transmission** — infectious agent, reservoir, portal of exit, mode of transmission (direct, indirect, droplet, airborne), portal of entry, susceptible host; breaking the chain
48. **Standard Precautions & Hand Hygiene** — universal vs. standard precautions, handwashing technique, alcohol-based hand rub, when to wash vs. sanitize, fingernail policies, jewelry policies
49. **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)** — gloves (exam, utility, sterile), masks (surgical, N95), protective eyewear, face shields, gowns; donning/doffing sequence, when to change
50. **Vaccination & Immunization Requirements** — hepatitis B series, influenza, MMR, varicella, Tdap; declination policy, titer testing, post-exposure prophylaxis

### II. Prevention of Cross-Contamination (34%)

51. **Surface Disinfection Protocols** — EPA-registered disinfectants, tuberculocidal vs. hospital-grade, contact time, spray-wipe-spray technique, classification of surfaces (clinical contact vs. housekeeping)
52. **Barrier Techniques for Clinical Surfaces** — when to use barriers vs. disinfection, barrier materials, proper placement and removal, surfaces requiring barriers
53. **Dental Unit Waterline Management** — biofilm formation, EPA water quality standards (<500 CFU/mL), flushing protocols, waterline treatment products, independent water reservoirs, testing methods
54. **Single-Use Items & Disposables** — items that cannot be reprocessed, sharps handling, proper disposal, identifying single-use vs. reusable items
55. **Laboratory Asepsis** — disinfection of impressions, prosthetics, and appliances; communication with dental labs; incoming/outgoing item protocols
56. **Radiography-Specific Infection Control** — cross-domain review of aseptic radiography workflow, film vs. sensor handling, panoramic unit disinfection

### III. Process Instruments and Devices (26%)

57. *****Spaulding Classification & Instrument Processing** — critical, semi-critical, non-critical categories; processing requirements for each; the handpiece exception *(already built)*
58. **Instrument Cleaning Methods** — hand scrubbing, ultrasonic cleaning, instrument washers; PPE for cleaning; holding solutions; inspection before sterilization
59. **Sterilization Methods** — steam autoclave (gravity, pre-vacuum), chemical vapor (chemiclave), dry heat (static, forced air), ethylene oxide; parameters, advantages, limitations for each
60. **Sterilization Monitoring** — biological indicators (spore tests), chemical indicators (internal/external), process indicators; frequency of monitoring; positive spore test protocol
61. **Instrument Packaging & Storage** — wrapping methods (paper, nylon, pouches), package labeling (date, sterilizer, cycle), event-related vs. time-related shelf life, proper storage conditions
62. **Handpiece & Semicritical Device Processing** — handpiece internal contamination, lubrication protocols, heat sterilization requirements, items requiring special processing (ultrasonic tips, air/water syringe tips)

### IV. Occupational Safety and Administration (20%)

63. **OSHA Bloodborne Pathogens Standard** — exposure control plan, exposure determination, universal precautions requirement, post-exposure evaluation, Sharps Injury Log, annual training
64. **Exposure Incident Management** — needlestick protocol, mucous membrane exposure, source patient testing, PEP decision algorithm, documentation, follow-up schedule
65. **Hazard Communication (HazCom/GHS)** — Safety Data Sheets (SDS), GHS labeling, chemical inventory, employee right-to-know, pictograms, storage requirements
66. **Waste Management & Regulated Medical Waste** — classification of waste (general, regulated medical, hazardous, pharmaceutical), sharps containers, biohazard bags, amalgam waste, lead foil recycling, manifest tracking
67. **Ergonomics & Injury Prevention** — musculoskeletal disorders, neutral positioning, stretching protocols, proper lifting technique, repetitive strain prevention
68. **Recordkeeping & Compliance Programs** — OSHA 300 Log, training documentation, exposure records retention (30 years), infection control coordinator role, annual program review
